Welcome to the Ultra-Phone Game!

You are the CEO of a mobile phone company competing in the market.

Setting the Stage

Your company and your competitor are deciding on pricing for a new mobile device.

You can set a high price or a low price.

The market has two segments: one that will pay a high price but prefers low, and another that only pays the low price.

Potential Outcomes

Let's explore the potential outcomes based on different pricing strategies.

Both Price High

Firm A: $800 million

Firm B: $800 million

Both Price Low

Firm A: $600 million

Firm B: $600 million

You Price High, Competitor Prices Low

Firm A: $0

Firm B: $1.2 billion

You Price Low, Competitor Prices High

Firm A: $1.2 billion

Firm B: $0

Make Your Decision

What price will you set for your phone?

Results

Conclusion and Lesson

This game only has one Nash equilibrium, where both companies choosing to set a low price is the most stable strategy, much like the scenario in the classic Prisoner's Dilemma.

Reference: Praveen K. Kopalle and Robert A. Shumsky, "Game Theory Models of Pricing," in The Oxford Handbook of Pricing Management, 2012.
